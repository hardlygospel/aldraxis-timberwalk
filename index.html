Player Character Reference

Aldraxis Timberwalk

Wood Elf · Druid · Circle of Spores

Wood Elf Druid Circle of Spores Wisdom Half Caster of Decay Mask of the Wild

Where the mycelium meets the moss-line, Aldraxis listens. To the wood elves of his grove he is the threshold-warden — he shepherds the dying back into the soil, and the spores back into the canopy. The Circle of Spores taught him that the forest is not made of trees, but of the quiet network beneath them. His staff is rooted; his armour weeps the faint lavender bloom of luminous fungus; his enemies fall, then rise again at his quiet command.

🍄 Circle of Spores Features

LV 2Halo of Spores

As a reaction when a creature you can see moves into a space within 10 ft of you, or starts its turn there, force a Constitution save against your spell save DC. On a failure: necrotic damage equal to:

1d4 (lv 2) → 1d6 (lv 6) → 1d8 (lv 10) → 1d10 (lv 14)

LV 2Symbiotic Entity

Use a Wild Shape charge as an action (instead of transforming) to empower yourself for 10 minutes:

  • Gain 4 × druid level temporary HP
  • Halo of Spores damage doubles (rolls twice)
  • Melee weapon attacks deal an extra 1d6 necrotic

Ends if you Wild-Shape into a beast or all temp HP is lost.

LV 6Fungal Infestation

When a beast or humanoid you can see within 10 ft dies, use a reaction to animate it as a zombie for 1 hour. The zombie acts on your turn, takes one action you mentally command, and counts toward Halo of Spores' aura.

Uses = Wisdom modifier (min 1) per long rest.

LV 10Spreading Spores

While Symbiotic Entity is active, use a bonus action to hurl your Halo of Spores aura into a 10-ft cube within 30 ft. The cube becomes your Halo for 1 minute (or until you move it again). Costs no resources beyond the bonus action.

LV 14Fungal Body

You become immune to being blinded, deafened, frightened, and poisoned. Critical hits against you are treated as ordinary hits. Aldraxis is, at this point, more colony than elf.

🐾 Wild Shape Reference

Wild Shape twice per short rest (regains on short and long). Stats become the beast's; mental stats remain yours. Hit points are tracked separately — when WS drops to 0, you revert with overflow damage applied to your real HP.

Druid Lv Max CR Limitations Sample Forms
2¼ No flying or swimming speed Wolf, giant badger, mastiff, panther, riding horse
4½ No flying speed Crocodile, giant wolf spider, ape (cr ½)
81 No restrictions Giant eagle, dire wolf, giant elk, brown bear (cr 1)

Symbiotic Entity vs. Wild Shape

You can spend a Wild Shape charge on either. Symbiotic Entity is the typical Spore Druid combat opener — temp HP scaling with druid level + extra necrotic damage means the spore form usually beats a beast form for damage and survivability at low-to-mid tier.

⚔ Combat Quick Reference

Typical Opening (Tier 2, lv 5–10)

  1. Bonus action: activate Symbiotic Entity (4×lv temp HP)
  2. Action: cast a control spell (Spike Growth, Moonbeam)
  3. Reaction: Halo of Spores on enemies in range
  4. Subsequent turns: melee with Shillelagh + 1d6 necrotic, or sustain control spell

Reaction Economy

Spore Druids have one of the densest reaction kits in 5e — every 10-ft trigger fires Halo of Spores, but only one reaction per round. Use other reactions (e.g., Absorb Elements) thoughtfully.

Necrotic Stack at lv 6 (Symbiotic active)

Halo: 2d6 necrotic on save fail
Melee: weapon + 1d6 necrotic
Cantrip: chill touch 1d8 necrotic
